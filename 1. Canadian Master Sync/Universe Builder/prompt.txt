Classify this company into EXACTLY ONE of the following types:

IN UNIVERSE:

- Miner
  Any company that mines, explores for, or develops mineral/metal deposits.
  Includes: junior explorers with no revenue yet (they will have mineral property descriptions,
  drilling results, resource estimates, or exploration programs in their filings).
  Includes: uranium miners, coal miners, lithium/battery metal developers.
  Does NOT include oil & gas companies (even if they hold minor mineral claims).

- Royalty & Streaming
  Companies that provide upfront capital to miners in exchange for a royalty (% of revenue or
  production) OR a stream (right to buy metal at a fixed, below-market price). They do NOT
  operate mines themselves - they collect payments from mine operators.
  Key signal: revenue described as "royalty income", "stream deliveries", or "attributable
  production" from properties they do not operate. Examples: Franco-Nevada, Wheaton Precious
  Metals, Altius Minerals, Osisko Gold Royalties.
  Identify the primary commodity focus of the portfolio.

- Mining Services
  Companies providing services TO miners: contract mining, drilling, blasting, assaying/lab
  testing, mine engineering, tailings management, environmental services for mines, mine safety
  technology. The company's primary customers must be mining companies.

- Mining Technology
  Software, AI, autonomous systems, sensors, or instruments developed SPECIFICALLY for mining
  operations. NOT general tech companies that happen to sell to miners.

- Metals Extraction
  Companies focused on extracting/processing metals from ore, tailings, recycled material, or
  secondary sources (smelters, refiners, processors). Distinct from Miners who extract from
  the ground.

- Mining Investment Company
  An actively managed company or vehicle whose PRIMARY purpose is capital allocation into
  mining assets or companies. Must make active investment decisions (not a passive ETF or
  index fund). Examples: merchant banks focused on mining, actively managed mining holding
  companies.

- Mining Finance
  Financial institutions whose CORE business is financing or advising the mining industry.
  Must be primarily/specifically mining-focused. General banks that happen to have mining
  clients do NOT qualify.

- Conglomerate
  A large diversified company with a SIGNIFICANT mining division (>20% of revenue/assets)
  alongside other major business lines. Very rare - only if mining is genuinely material.

OUT OF UNIVERSE:

- Not Mining
  Clearly unrelated to mining: oil & gas companies, banks, tech, retail, pharma, real estate,
  clean energy without mining exposure, general industrials, etc.
  Oil & gas companies with minor mineral claims (not material to the business) → Not Mining.

- Uncertain
  Use ONLY when:
  (a) The filing text is insufficient to make a confident determination, OR
  (b) The company is primarily oil & gas BUT has SIGNIFICANT mining/mineral assets that
      represent a material part of the business (e.g. >30% of assets/revenue). In this case,
      explain the O&G/mining split.
  ALWAYS explain specifically WHY you are uncertain in the reasoning field.
