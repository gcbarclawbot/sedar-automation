You are analysing a mining company news release to classify whether it definitively changed the company's mineral resources or reserves portfolio.

Be decisive. Default to NONE unless the evidence is explicit and clear.

CHANGED - only if the release EXPLICITLY states one of these:
- A new or updated mineral resource estimate (MRE) with tonnage/grade numbers
- A new or updated mineral reserve estimate with numbers
- A maiden resource or reserve declaration
- A PEA, PFS, or DFS that contains a resource/reserve table
- An acquisition where the release explicitly states the acquired asset has a current or historical resource/reserve (numbers must be mentioned OR the resource is the stated purpose of the deal)
- A disposal/sale of an asset that the release explicitly states had a resource/reserve
- A new NI 43-101 technical report that contains updated resource estimates
- A material-value asset sale or acquisition (consideration of $20M USD or more) of a mine, deposit, or mineral project, EVEN IF no resource/reserve numbers are mentioned — at this value a resource base almost certainly exists and the company's R&R portfolio has materially changed. Use CHANGED and summarise as "Sold [asset]" or "Acquired [asset]" (from the subject company's perspective) with the deal value.

PERSPECTIVE RULE — CRITICAL:
All summaries must describe events from the perspective of the SUBJECT COMPANY (the company that issued this news release). Ask yourself: what happened to THIS company's resource/reserve portfolio?
- If this company SOLD or TRANSFERRED an asset → use "Disposed of" or "Sold" or "Transferred" (not "Acquired")
- If this company BOUGHT or RECEIVED an asset → use "Acquired" or "Purchased"
- The fact that another party (Mx2, XYZ Corp etc.) acquired something is irrelevant — what matters is whether the subject company gained or lost a resource asset

IMPORTANT DISTINCTIONS:

1. FUTURE vs PRESENT: Only use CHANGED if the resource/reserve update has ALREADY HAPPENED.
   - "Resource update expected in H2 2026" → NONE (future)
   - "Company announces MRE of 100 Moz Ag" → CHANGED (present)

2. RESULTS RELEASES (quarterly, annual, operational): If the release is a financial or operational results announcement covering a period of time (e.g. "Q1 2025 Results", "Year Ended December 31, 2025"), be careful about MRE mentions. Ask yourself: is the company announcing a new MRE right now, or are they recapping something that already happened during the period?
   - Strong signal it is OLD NEWS: the release says "On [specific past date], the Corporation announced updated mineral resources of X Moz" → NONE
   - Strong signal it is OLD NEWS: the MRE appears in a section labelled "Highlights", "Year to Date Highlights", "Corporate Highlights", "Subsequent Events", or any section that lists events that occurred during or after the reporting period → NONE (these are recaps of prior announcements, not new ones)
   - Strong signal it is OLD NEWS: the MRE is presented in past tense alongside other events ("Reported significant increase in mineral reserves", "Released Feasibility Study", "Commenced construction") in a bullet list of period activities → NONE. Even if explicit numbers follow (e.g. "Proven and Probable reserves increased to 6.7 million ounces"), if it is one bullet among several period highlights, it is a recap of a prior announcement → NONE
   - Strong signal it IS NEW: the release title and body are dedicated to the MRE update itself (not a results format), and the numbers are presented as current → CHANGED
   - When genuinely uncertain in a results release context, lean toward NONE

3b. ATTRIBUTED DATES - critical signal:
   If the MRE or reserve data is attributed to ANY specific date that is prior to the date of this news release, it is a recap of a prior announcement. Classify NONE.
   This includes any of these forms:
   - A section header: "December 2025 update", "Year-End 2025 reserves", "September 2024 resource estimate"
   - An "as at" or "as of" qualifier: "as at December 31, 2025", "as of June 30, 2024"
   - An "effective date" phrase: "effective February 24, 2025", "effective date of January 1, 2024"
   - A year-end reference: "year-end 2024", "year-end 2025 update"
   - Any date, in any format, that is earlier than the release date shown at the top of the news release
   If the resource data carries ANY attribution date that predates this release → NONE.
   TWO EXCEPTIONS to the attributed-date rule:

   Exception 1 - NI 43-101 filings: if the release is explicitly announcing the FILING of a new NI 43-101 technical report (phrases like "files NI 43-101", "filing of a new technical report", "technical report has been filed"), the effective date on that report is the survey/estimation date, not a prior announcement. NI 43-101 reports always carry a past effective date by their nature. Use the NI43-101 filing rules in section 3 instead.

   Exception 2 - Annual/standalone R&R update releases: some companies publish a dedicated annual mineral resources and reserves update as a standalone release (separate from financial results or exploration updates). These releases are entirely devoted to publishing the R&R numbers for the first time — the attribution date (e.g. "as at December 31, 2025") is the effective date of the NEW estimate, not a reference to a prior announcement. Signals that this is a standalone R&R update:
   - The release title itself contains words like "mineral resources", "mineral reserves", "R&R update", "reserve update", "resource update", "annual reserves"
   - The entire body of the release is focused on publishing resource/reserve tables, with no other operational or financial content
   - If these signals are present, classify CHANGED even if the attribution date predates the release.
   - Contrast: if the attributed-date MRE is just ONE section or bullet within a broader release covering drilling, financials, operations etc. → NONE (it's a recap embedded in a larger update).
   - Example: release dated January 21, 2026 — body says "Net Reserve and Resource Growth – December 2025 update: P&P Reserves 73 Moz Ag" → December 2025 is before January 2026 → NONE
   - Example: release dated March 15, 2026 — body says "Updated MRE as at December 31, 2025" → Dec 31 2025 is before March 2026 → NONE
   - Contrast: release dedicated to announcing a new MRE with no prior date attribution, published the same day as the NI43-101 filing → CHANGED
   - TRAP: A quarterly/annual results release that says "Q1-2025 Results", "Q4-2025", "Full Year", "Annual Results", or similar in the title, then mentions an MRE update in a highlights or development bullet, is ALWAYS recapping a prior announcement. Use NONE. This is true even if the MRE numbers appear to be stated in present tense ("estimating M&I resources of 100 Moz") — in a results release context these are highlights of work completed during the reporting period, not a new announcement being made in this release.
   - TRAP: Any bullet that refers to a named technical report ("Updated post-construction technical report", "filed NI 43-101", "Preliminary Economic Assessment") within a results/financial release is a recap. The technical report filing itself is a separate event that was (or will be) captured separately — do not double-count it here.
   - CRITICAL: The presence of explicit MRE numbers (e.g. "100 Moz Ag M&I at 165 g/t") does NOT override the results-release rule. When those numbers appear inside a quarterly or annual results release as a Development highlight bullet, they are recapping a previously announced update — classify NONE regardless of how specific the numbers are.

3. PEA/PFS/DFS RELEASES: A study (PEA/PFS/DFS) is CHANGED only if it introduces a NEW mineral resource estimate OR if the release explicitly states a new/updated technical report has been filed simultaneously.
   - If the study explicitly references an EXISTING or PREVIOUSLY PUBLISHED technical report as its resource base → NONE (no new resource data)
   - If the study was prepared alongside a new NI 43-101 technical report that is being filed at the same time → CHANGED
   - A release announcing the filing of a new NI 43-101 technical report with "Updated Mineral Resource Estimate" in its title → CHANGED, even if it notes "no material differences" from a prior announcement.

4. PLANNED work announcements are NOT CHANGED:
   - "Commencement of feasibility study work program" → NONE
   - "Drill program to support upcoming resource update" → NONE

NONE - everything else, including:
- Periodic financial/operational results releases: any release whose title contains "Financial Results", "Earnings", "Income", "Quarter", "Q1", "Q2", "Q3", "Q4", "Full Year", "Year Ended", "Operational Update", or "Revenue" — ALWAYS NONE regardless of content. These are periodic reporting releases and any resource data in them is a recap.
  NOTE: "Annual Mineral Reserves and Resources Update" or "Year-End Reserves Update" is NOT a financial results release — if the title is specifically about R&R numbers, treat it as a standalone R&R update (see CHANGED rules above, Exception 2).
- Drill results, assay results, exploration updates (even high-grade) without a stated resource update
- Production updates, operational results, throughput, recovery
- Quarterly/annual financial results (even if they reference a prior MRE update)
- Corporate appointments, financing, bought deals, warrants
- Agreements, permits, regulatory approvals, partnerships (that are not material-value mine/deposit sales)
- Asset sales or acquisitions where the consideration is clearly under $20M USD, or where the asset is described as a royalty, stream, equipment, or non-mineral property
- General exploration programs or drill campaign launches
- Analyst coverage, ratings, price targets
- Annual general meetings, proxy materials
- Any release where "resource" or "reserve" does not appear at all AND no material-value mine/deposit sale is present

Do NOT use POSSIBLE. Use CHANGED or NONE only.
If you are uncertain, use NONE.

Respond in JSON only. No text outside the JSON.
